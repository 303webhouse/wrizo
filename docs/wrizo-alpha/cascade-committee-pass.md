# The Cascade — committee double-pass · 2026-07-17

**Place at:** `docs/wrizo-alpha/cascade-committee-pass.md`.
**Commissioned by Nick from the desktop sitting:** the left drawer
becomes a pop-out cascade from a persistent vertical strip on the
FAR RIGHT — categories (Journal, Page, Plan, Drawers, Shelf, maybe
others) each opening a panel (layer 2), with a third pop-out (layer 3)

Mandate: Architects review the idea first; then both passes on what

## ARCHITECTS' REVIEW (the commissioned first step)

**Verdict: sound, and better than sound — it completes the ranges.**
The strip is the **glance** (persistent, always one look away). The
category panel is the **reach** (transient, drawer-pull semantics
carried forward whole). The third layer is a range the canon never
named: **survey** — seeing a container's contents without traveling
into it. A writer surveys a board's cards while the page keeps focus;
travel remains a deliberate act. Glance · reach · survey · travel.
The gaze-and-flight ladder gains its missing rung.

Mechanics: the strip persists below the top bar (Done keeps the
corner; no right-edge collision with FX3's right-aligned modes).
Layers 2–3 are overlays — the paper NEVER reflows (standing law) —
and both dissolve on keystroke via the one vanishing engine; the
strip remains, like the grip. Layer 3 opens automatically from a
layer-2 choice; it is never something to discover. At laptop widths
the layers cap and may overlap each other (never the text measure).
Keyboard: the strip is focusable; each layer traps sensibly; Escape
walks back one layer. Left side after the move: paper and sliver
only — the freed width feeds FX3's larger paper directly.

**One reconciliation the Architects flag as the pass's biggest call:**
yesterday reserved the right track for AB4's Wall. The cascade now
owns the right edge — and Nick's own Plan example (cards as large
thumbnails beside a focused page) IS the Wall's browsing posture.
Recommendation: **AB4 re-scopes onto the cascade** — the survey layer
becomes how boards and walls are browsed; pinning and threads build
ON this system as the next arc ticket; the reserved corkboard track
retires from the plan. One right edge, one system.

## PASS ONE — layer contents (minimum visible, expansion on demand)

**The strip (glance):** Page · Journal · Plan · Drawers · Shelf, top
to bottom — home base first, then reach-frequency order. No Library
until a Library exists; no Settings (the sliver's foot owns that,
per FX3); room reserved for future places. Icons + engraved labels;
olive marks the open category.

**Layer 2 (reach), per category:**
- **Page** — the Page face carried forward whole: title, star, home
  line always visible; tags and the sending verbs (Move/Copy, Port)
  as expanding rows; the saved-silently footer.
- **Journal** — two verbs (Open the Journal — travel; New page) plus
  a short recent list; "All pages →" opens the survey.
- **Plan** — Create a Board · Plot a Story · Open… (opens the survey:
  the board list). Move and Delete live on each board's row behind a
  quiet disclosure, not at top level — minimum options, and
  destructive verbs never sit in first position.
- **Drawers** — the drawer list; choosing one opens its survey
  (filed pages).
- **Shelf** — the shelved list; survey previews on selection.

**Layer 3 (survey):** large thumbnails, title + a two-line excerpt or
card image, current item marked olive; click = travel (mounts it in
the main area); drag-to-reorder only where an order-surface lawfully
exists (a board's own cards — one-order-surface principle holds). No
counts, no badges, nothing begs.

**Destructive verbs (house pattern, recorded):** Delete exists —
writers own their content — behind a disclosure, one plain confirm,
with "Move to the Shelf instead" offered in the same breath.
Destructive color appears only inside the confirm, never at rest.

**Instruments panel (refining FX3's stub):** per-instrument on/off
(progress bar; glow), unit — words / lines / time (time rides the
session timer), target value. Lines stay the default. All writer
agency; the instruments themselves remain verdict-free.

## PASS TWO — opposition and trims

**Skeptic-writer:** *Three layers is depth anxiety.* — Layers are
spatial, not hierarchical menus: the strip never moves, each layer
sits beside its parent, Escape walks back, a keystroke clears it all.
*Right-side navigation is unfamiliar.* — Inspectors and panels live
right in every studio tool (design apps, IDEs); the writing hand owns
the left, the reaching hand the right. *Thumbnails invite endless
browsing.* — The survey opens only on request and dies on the first
keystroke; the page holds focus by law. **Growth vs. brand:** Growth
wanted the survey richer (covers, colors, drag-anywhere); Brand held
the quiet — trimmed to typographic thumbnails, image only where a
card IS an image. **Trims:** no pinned/favorite rows in layer 2 (the
star already exists on Page); no per-category settings; no strip
customization this ticket; breadcrumbs rejected (spatial layout makes
them redundant).
