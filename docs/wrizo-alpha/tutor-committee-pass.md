# The Tutor — committee double-pass · 2026-07-18

**Place at:** `docs/wrizo-alpha/tutor-committee-pass.md`.
**Commissioned by Nick:** the AI assistance panel, named the Tutor —
the right-hand surface where the writer goes when they don't know
what they need, opposite the cascade's drawers for when they do.
Part chatbot, part mentor, part programmatic, part AI-assisted —
never a ghostwriter. Nudges, gentle guidance, unblocking.

---

## PASS ONE — proposals

### Experts (the why)

Nick's two-sides law is the frame, and it is exactly right: the
cascade maps the writer's KNOWN needs (organized, named, striated —
you open a drawer because you know what's in it); the Tutor is where
the writer meets the UNKNOWN (the block, the doubt, the question
without a shape). One side is the desk's furniture; the other is the
mentor's chair beside it. The thesis holds the line: writing is a
process, and the Tutor is the process's companion — it makes the
writer do the work, which is why "Tutor" is the correct name over
Mentor or Editor. A tutor never writes your essay.

**The ghostwriter rail, made precise — the pass's center:**
- **The Tutor speaks ABOUT the writing, never AS it.** It may point,
  name, and question; it may never phrase. "This sentence's tense
  disagrees with the scene" is tutoring; "here's the fixed
  sentence" is ghostwriting.
- **Reference yes, composition never.** A list of period-accurate
  side-character names is a baby-name book on the desk — reference
  atoms, lawful (and it kills the research rabbit hole Nick named).
  A character description, a line of dialogue, a sentence for the
  work — composition, never, regardless of how small. When asked,
  the Tutor declines in character and redirects to a question that
  sends the writer back to the page.
- **No Tutor output enters a writing surface by any affordance.** No
  insert, no apply, no copy-into-page. The future anti-slop paste
  rail will enforce it mechanically; until then the affordances
  simply do not exist and the Tutor's register produces questions
  and observations, not paste-worthy prose.

**Nudges vs. anti-solicitation — the reconciliation:** the Tutor may
WRITE but never CALL. Nick's words — "when they need a nudge, it
happens HERE" — resolve it: nudges are letters waiting in the
Tutor's room, found on the writer's approach. No badges, no toasts,
no counts, no interruptions, no presence dots. **The room never
knocks.** The Tutor is a place you go, not a voice that reaches you.

### Architects (the how)

1. **Geometry — the mirror of the sliver.** A grip at the paper's
   RIGHT edge (the FX2 clearance law mirrored: persistent chrome
   never enters the measure), opening a ~300px panel rightward into
   the margin. The paper never moves, any width; at narrow widths
   the open panel overlays per the CD2 small-screen law.
2. **The vanishing law with the dock rider, inherited whole.**
   Transient by default — the Tutor dissolves on the first keystroke,
   which IS the product thesis: the mentor steps back the moment
   writing resumes. Dockable by the writer's deliberate word for
   working through a block. Escape, close, same grammar as the
   cascade's survey.
3. **The brain is layered — programmatic first, model second.**
   Lens row (quiet verbs, minimum options): **Consistency** (name/
   spelling scan across the project — deterministic, offline,
   private), **Structure** (where this page sits against the Plan's
   boards — existing data, no model), **Fragments** (the writer's
   own captures resurfaced — recency and tags in v1, honest about
   its simplicity), and the open **conversation** (model-assisted
   craft dialogue, writer-initiated only).
4. **Nudge engine v1 is programmatic:** templated observations keyed
   to real state (a starred page untouched for days; a board with an
   empty act), written in the Tutor's voice, placed in the panel,
   never pushed. Model-written nudges are a later question.
5. **Context is per-page in v1.** The Tutor talks about THIS page;
   a project-level room is a later ticket. Threads persist.
6. **SCHEMA FLAG, loud:** persisted Tutor threads are the arc's
   second schema addition. **The Tutor ticket carries NO merge
   pre-authorization — Nick's explicit go required** (the corrected
   rule's first scheduled use). Local-first store + sync, additive,
   grandfathered like origin.
7. **Model plumbing:** server-side proxy only (no keys client-side);
   writer-initiated calls only (no ambient spend); a plain one-time
   disclosure on first open ("what you ask the Tutor travels to a
   language model; your pages stay yours"); offline, the programmatic
   lenses still work and the panel says so quietly.
8. **Voice:** one voice now — warm, brief, question-forward — every
   string through deskLexicon. Theme dialects (Flux's glitch-tutor,
   Volant's near-wordless one) are the theme arc's delight, later.

### Marketing (growth · brand)

Brand: the Tutor IS the anti-slop position made visible — the AI
that never writes your book, in a market of ghostwriter buttons. The
refusal is the feature; say it on the landing page. Growth: the demo
moment is asking it for a paragraph and watching it decline
beautifully, then unblock you with a question that actually works.
The Tutor gives the social/collaboration future its trust floor:
everything on Wrizo was written by a person.

## PASS TWO — opposition and trims

**Skeptic-writer:** *An AI reading my novel.* — Writer-initiated
only; programmatic lenses never leave the device; the disclosure is
plain; nothing ambient. *It will nag me.* — The room never knocks;
the law has no exceptions and no badge. *If it can't write, it's
useless.* — The deterministic lenses do real work on day one;
consistency scans alone earn the panel. *I'll lean on it instead of
writing.* — It dissolves on your first keystroke and its register
pushes you back to the page; dependency is designed against, not
just discouraged. **Growth vs. brand:** Growth wanted proactive
surfacing and richer generation at the edges; Brand held both lines
— trimmed to the room-never-knocks law and the composition rail,
absolute. **Trims:** no project-level room in v1; no model-written
nudges in v1; no Tutor on the threshold (first-run stays pure —
progressive disclosure finds the Tutor later); no theme dialects
yet; no voice/audio anything.
