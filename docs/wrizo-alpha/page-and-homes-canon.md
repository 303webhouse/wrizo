## The vision in one paragraph

There is one Page. It is the home base — the same paper everywhere,
learned once by the hands. Everything about a page is sought, never
shown: its identity lives in a drawer. Where a page belongs is decided
by the door the writer came through, never by the app's habit. Pages
join walls and books and journals as members, not captives. The writer
may glance right, reach into a drawer, look up at the wall, or travel —
each one deliberate act apart. And the page itself is a page: it fills,
it turns, it never becomes a scroll.

## The eight laws

**Law 1 — One Page, and Page means Document.** The Page is the single
writing primitive. It may hold a sentence or a novel; its length lives
behind one doorway (see Law 8). A Page can be a one-page chapter filed
into a Book in a Drawer, a loose thought, a screenplay, a bibliography —
same primitive, different dress and home. The paper's texture and
measure are the one inviolable constant across every location and
theme: the writer's body learns one surface.

**Law 2 — Provenance sets the home; the mode sets the tools.** These
axes never conflate again. Enter through the Journal: the page homes in
the Journal and wears journal furniture (timer, progress, typewriter,
ink, the forward lock — the parked flourishes return as *Journal
furniture*, not universal meters). Enter through a project door ("new
screenplay"): the page homes in that project; the Journal never sees
it. Enter through the home base: the page is loose — *belongs nowhere
yet* is a legitimate, permanent home — and starting there never files
it. Free Write is a posture available on any page; it files nothing.

**Law 3 — Membership, not capture.** A page sent onward from the
Journal keeps its place in the Journal; the Journal forgets nothing. A
page pinned to a wall still lives in its chapter. One page, many
memberships, one identity — and the Page drawer always tells the truth
about all of them. (The one substrate-adjacent design here: membership
semantics ride existing home/link fields where possible; the AB3 brief
carries the exact mapping.)

**Law 4 — One drawer, one size, contents flip.** The left panel is a
single physical drawer of fixed geometry (per theme). At rest it holds
the mode's tools. Pull **Page** and the same drawer flips to identity:
title, star, tags, *where it lives* (all memberships), and the sending
verbs (Move / Copy / Pin / Port). Pull **Plan** at the wall and it
holds the wall's kit. Contents slide — the desk-drawer motion — the
geometry never moves. Everything that cluttered the space under the
paper lives here now; beneath the paper is desk. The saved-silently
line becomes the drawer's quiet footer. Progressive disclosure *within*
the drawer (which items hide behind toggles) is ruled paint: it rides
the detail pass after the plumbing, per Nick's deferral and Fable's
concurrence. The absorbed canon's Places panels (Journal, Shelf,
Drawers reached from the rail) share this same drawer body and all its
guardrails.

**Law 5 — The wall: the writer may look up.** Plan on the rail swaps
the stage from the page to a wall — the single sanctioned exception to
page primacy, with the way back always one tap. Walls are plural and
toggleable per project (a plot-structure wall beside a research wall).
A wall holds anything: cards, beats, outline pages, pages that live
elsewhere, imported images, ink drawn in the Journal — and **threads**
connect them (the string on the corkboard; casting reference → character
card → the scene already written). One click selects and offers verbs;
**double-click opens** — the home page travels through any wall item to
the thing itself, way back guaranteed. Anything on a wall can move into
any page. Walls reorganize freely; nothing on a wall solicits.

**Law 6 — The measure law, two classes.** A **Page's** measure is law:
never the writer's to drag, identical everywhere. A **Card's** measure
is entirely the writer's — resized and shaped at will within the room
the drawer, the corkboard, and the future assist need. The comic
artist's wide cell is the proof case: drawn in the Journal's own ink,
shaped on the wall, threaded in sequence, assembled at Publish.

**Law 7 — Assembly at Publish.** Thinking-space holds media freely;
the page admits it only at assembly time. Images live on walls without
ceremony; inserting an image *into a page* — and sequencing pages or
cells into a finished artifact — is Publish-mode work. Drafting stays
pure words. The anti-slop wall is untouched and text-scoped: reference
images are creative material, not ghostwritten prose; words still flow
out freely and never in.

**Law 8 — The sheet law: the scroll dies.** A Page is made of
**sheets** — derived, never stored: sheet boundaries are computed from
the one true text by capacity (the prose measure's line budget; the
screenplay's courier convention at the industry's ~55 lines, making
script page-counts professionally real). Writing fills a sheet and
turns it to a fresh blank one. Editing mid-document re-settles the
sheets forward automatically. **Typewriter on:** the writer never sees
the bottom — lines rise and fade off the top as today, sheets turning
silently beneath one seamless stream. **Typewriter off:** the document
stands as separate, Word-like sheets, and the mouse wheel *turns*
sheets rather than scrolling; navigation reuses the notebook grammar
the Journal already taught (‹ 3 / 7 ›). Boards and Cards are exempt
(Law 6's free class). A manual sheet-break convention may join the
markdown set later; not v1. This law completes containment
structurally: there is no longer any such thing as a page without an
edge.

## The four ranges of attention (absorbing the old canon's three)

**Glance** (the pinned card at the corkboard — zero acts) · **Reach**
(the drawer flips; Places panels — one to two acts; panels dissolve on
a keystroke, run one level deep, stay quiet) · **Look up** (the wall
replaces the page — one act there, one act back) · **Travel** (the
rooms — always exactly one deliberate act more than reaching). Both
flight doorways from the absorbed canon stand: grow-a-plan from any
unplanned page; open-or-create the page from any beat.
**Availability, never invitation** binds every doorway, drawer, and
wall.

## Vocabulary (proposed; lexicon-seam territory)

Canonical terms: **Page** (the primitive; capital in docs), **sheet**
(the derived visual unit), **Card** (the free-measure class),
**wall** (a board worn as Plan). Themes dress the words (Plateau may
prefer "leaves" for sheets; the seam exists for exactly this). The
data model's `journal entries` naming is historical; no rename is
required for the laws to hold.

## What themes may never touch

The eight laws, the four ranges, the paper constant, the anti-
solicitation law, and the orange/olive lane laws — grammar, invariant
across all five territories. Everything visual — the drawer's slide,
the wall's cork, the sheet-turn's motion — is theme dress.

## The honest build line and the proposed ladder

Nearly everything above stands on rails already built: the drawer
(DeskFrame's fixed track + the lexicon seams), homes (existing
home/project fields; creation-flow changes), walls (boards exist;
plural = a picker; threads = links inside the board's own data), ink
import (the Port pipe shipped in the J-arc), double-click travel (the
way-back system), sheets (a derived view over `entry.text` — zero
schema). **The one genuinely new plumbing is imported images:** binary
storage, local-first blobs, sync, size limits — its own pass, not a
hand-wave.

Proposed tickets, in order (Nick rules the order with the word):

- **AB3 — the Drawer and the Homes.** Law 4 whole (Tools/Page flip,
  Places panels), Law 2's creation flows and defaults, Law 3's
  membership surfaced in the drawer, the metadata clutter retired from
  under every paper. The corkboard's pinned-card glance rides along
  where cheap.
- **AB4 — the Wall.** Law 5 whole minus images: plural walls, cards,
  page-membership pins, threads, double-click travel, journal-ink
  import, move-into-page. Law 6's card resizing.
- **AB5 — the Sheets.** Law 8 whole: derived pagination, the turn, the
  typewriter stream over silent turnover, wheel-turn, sheet nav; the
  screenplay convention.
- **I1 — the Image pass.** Storage/sync design first, then images on
  walls, then Law 7's Publish assembly (insertion and sequencing).

Each ticket keeps the house rhythm: one brief, decision-complete;
harness alongside; geometry floors from day one (the ab2.1 lesson);
report = push; Fable review; Nick's look closes.
