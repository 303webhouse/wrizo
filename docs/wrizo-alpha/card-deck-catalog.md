# The Card Deck Catalog — Experts' pass · 2026-07-20

**Charter:** Nick's commission of 2026-07-20 — brainstorm the Card
Decks that should exist across Wrizo's writer populations. A **Card
Deck** (Nick's coinage, now lexicon) is a preset: an optional wizard
that narrows choices, then deals a pre-built set of cards onto a
board in the writer's drawer, ready to be filled.

**Structural laws (bind every deck; precede the catalog):**
1. A deck deals ORDINARY cards — fully editable, movable,
   deletable, taggable; nothing locked, nothing mandatory (M1).
2. The blank board remains first-class; decks are invitations,
   never homework. No deck is ever suggested unprompted
   (anti-solicitation).
3. Wizards obey Nick's ratified rulings: opt-in, step-by-step,
   pop-out over the faded board, clickable-first, text permitted
   never required, ends on the dealt board, first card hints
   "Start Here" until any card is completed.
4. Deck DEFINITIONS are static app data (shipped with the app,
   zero schema, zero user-data changes); DEALING a deck is
   ordinary card creation on an ordinary board. Instantiated
   decks owe nothing to their template afterward.
5. Every deck names its narrowing choices (the wizard's questions)
   — few, concrete, clickable.

## The Fiction Room (literary & general narrative)
1. **Three-Act Structure** — the workhorse. Wizard: novel /
   novella / short story (deals proportionately). Cards: hook,
   inciting incident, first threshold; rising complications,
   midpoint reversal, darkest point; climax, resolution, final
   image.
2. **Save the Cat (Novel)** — the 15 beats for writers who want
   granularity. Wizard: full 15 / compressed 10.
3. **Hero's Journey** — Vogler's 12 stations. Wizard: full 12 /
   compressed 8 (for shorter arcs).
4. **Story Circle** — Harmon's 8 steps; the tightest loop for
   episodic or short work.
5. **Short Story** — premise, want vs. obstacle, the turn, image
   system, last line candidates. Small deck, big leverage.
6. **Character Study** — want vs. need, the wound, the
   contradiction, voice notes, relationship cards (threads shine:
   wire the cast together on the board).

## The Speculative Annex (worldbuilding & series)
7. **Worldbuilding** — rules of the world (magic/tech and their
   costs), deep history, places & maps, cultures & factions, power
   & economy, language notes, and the Iceberg card (what the
   reader never sees but the writer must know). Wizard: fantasy /
   SF / other (tunes card prompts only).
8. **Ensemble (spec-fic)** — protagonist, antagonist-with-a-point,
   foils, faction voices; dealt pre-threaded to the Worldbuilding
   deck's faction cards if both exist on the drawer.
9. **Series Bible** — canon facts, timeline, open threads,
   continuity questions. For book two and beyond.

## The Screen Room
10. **Feature Screenplay** — Save the Cat's 15 on the board.
    Wizard: feature / pilot (routes to 11).
11. **TV Pilot** — cold open, A/B/C story cards, act breaks
    (wizard: network 4-act / streaming 3-act), the season-engine
    card (what generates episode after episode).
12. **Eight Sequences** — the sequence method for writers who
    think in reels; alternate to 10.

## The Academy
13. **Thesis / Dissertation** — research question, lit-review
    clusters, methodology, chapter cards, evidence parking lot,
    citation ledger. Wizard: humanities / sciences (sciences
    variant deals IMRaD chapter shapes).
14. **Research Paper (IMRaD)** — intro, methods, results,
    discussion, abstract-last card (dealt with a note: write me
    final).
15. **Argumentative Essay** — claim, best counterclaim, three
    evidence cards, synthesis, so-what card. The student's deck.

## The Business Desk
16. **Résumé / CV** — wizard: field + career stage. Cards: summary
    line, achievement bullets (verb-first prompts), skills,
    tailoring card (paste the posting's language here — note:
    interacts with the paste rail's future design; flagged, not
    resolved).
17. **Business Proposal** — problem, solution, market, the ask,
    timeline, budget narrative, objections card.
18. **Grant Application** — need statement, objectives, methods,
    evaluation plan, budget justification, funder-alignment card
    (their words, your mission). Grants are the most
    board-shaped genre in business writing.
19. **Technical Doc** — audience card, prerequisites, task steps,
    troubleshooting, glossary. Wizard: how-to / reference.

## The Newsroom
20. **Feature Story** — nut graf, three lede candidates, source &
    quote cards, scene cards, kicker candidates.
21. **Investigation** — working hypothesis, document trail, source
    map (threads carry the web), timeline, fact-check ledger,
    legal-review card.

## The committee's v1 recommendation (B3's ship set)
One flagship per room, chosen for breadth of population and
demo-strength: **Three-Act Structure** (fiction), **Worldbuilding**
(the speculative annex's crown), **Feature Screenplay** (screen),
**Thesis/Dissertation** (academy), **Grant Application**
(business), **Feature Story** (newsroom) — six decks, plus
**Character Study** as the seventh if budget allows (it
demonstrates threads better than any other deck). The rest are the
library's second wave, already specified above. Nick ratifies the
ship set, cuts, or promotes at B3's brief.

**Two forward notes, flagged honestly:** the Résumé deck's
tailoring card touches the future paste-rail design (external text
inbound) — deferred to that rail's own reckoning, not resolved
here. And deck-to-deck threading (Ensemble ↔ Worldbuilding)
presumes both decks on one drawer's boards — a delight, not a
dependency; each deck stands alone.
