## PASS ONE — proposals

### Experts (the why)

The desk has four kinds of thing on it, and the current chrome mixes
them. **Hand tools** (format, structure, typewriter, ink, the lock) are
touched *while writing* — they belong at the hand, clipped to the
paper's edge like a palette to an easel. **Identity** (title, star,
tags, home, the sending verbs) is touched *about* the page, rarely and
deliberately — it belongs in the desk's drawer. **Places** (Journal,
Shelf, Drawers) are *reach* — also the drawer, per the ranges already
ruled. **The goal's instruments** (timer, progress, glow) are ambient —
they belong to the field, not to any menu. Today's far-left rail
duplicates Places, hosts a stub, and earns its column with nothing
unique but Catch. The writer's eye pays for four vertical systems;
the taxonomy needs three homes and a top line.

On the goal system: the glow must be **coverage, never a verdict** —
warmth that accumulates, never a state that judges. Absence of glow is
neutral (a page with no goal is just a page), never punitive. Measured
in lines because lines are the visual truth of every form — the poem,
the scene, the paragraph.

### Architects (the how)

1. **The sliver.** A slim grip riding the paper's left edge (visible
   hairline tab, glyph, olive when open; focusable; shortcut-bound).
   Opens a ~200px vertical strip **overlaying** the stage margin —
   never reflowing the paper (geometry law). Contents = the mode's
   hand tools: Draft's format/structure; Free Write's typewriter, ink
   (journal furniture where lawful), forward lock (mode furniture),
   capture items. Foot of the sliver = the goal block: timer numeral,
   2px progress hairline (Ruling 5's spec), a single inline goal edit.
   Keystroke dissolves it (the one vanishing engine); the grip
   remains. Boards' notecards exempt (Nick's rider).
2. **The drawer slims to two faces.** Page (identity) at rest —
   glancing left answers "what is this page, where does it live" —
   and Places. The tools face retires; its estate divides between the
   sliver (hand) and nothing (the drawer stops hosting tools).
   Fixed geometry unchanged; Law 4 amended accordingly.
3. **The far-left rail retires.** Places live in the drawer (the
   duplication ends); Library's stub leaves until a Library exists (no
   empty doors); **Catch rehomes to the top bar** as a quiet glyph-verb
   — one tap from anywhere, preserved. The DeskRail resume pointer's
   final home is the HB-arc's landing question (seam, named); interim,
   the drawer's Journal place face already lists recent pages.
4. **The top line.** Modes take it: FREE WRITE · DRAFT · REVISE ·
   WORKSHOP · PUBLISH, engraved register, olive hairline active —
   left-set. Right cluster: Catch · Done. **The top-bar title dies**:
   the paper names itself by its first line, and the drawer's Page
   face carries it — duplication was the only job the top title had.
5. **Composition on wide.** The frame (drawer | stage | wall-track)
   caps at a working max (fence: ~1720px) and centers; the desk-ground
   texture owns the outer field — a wide desk is more wood, not more
   furniture. **The corkboard track adopts the FX1 S5 law: render only
   with content** — it returns as the Wall's glance range in AB4.
   Paper centered in stage; the sliver lives in the margin that
   centering creates.
6. **The goal system.** One per-writer target in **line-equivalents at
   the paper's canonical measure** (deterministic, viewport-independent;
   a poem's short line counts 1, prose counts its wrapped lines at the
   canonical width — no device drift). Stored in writingSettings
   (zero-schema). A quiet default target ships (fence: 24 lines);
   clearing it disables both instruments. Instruments: the progress
   hairline + **the glow** — a warm radial behind the paper mapping to
   progress fraction, hard-capped (the field never burns; fence: the
   cap is a subtle halo, never a luminance event), easing to rest at
   full. **No numbers announced, no completion event, no color shift
   to "success" — fullness is the only arrival.** Timer/progress/glow
   render wherever a goal exists, any page.
7. **Loose pages open in Free Write** (origin==='loose' only; other
   untyped support pages keep Draft). The home-base blank gets the
   front-door posture.
8. **ToolRail retires entirely** if Script comes in scope (see T2):
   ScriptEditor gains drawer + sliver here, Board keeps its bare track
   until AB4. One system, no seams.

### Marketing (growth · brand)

Growth: the composed wide layout and the glow are the screenshot and
the demo moment; the sliver is a differentiator against toolbar-strip
editors; HB-arc's gate will *use* this glow — build once, teach at the
threshold. Brand: the desk metaphor deepens (hand / drawer / wall);
no AI buttons anywhere in the new chrome (the anti-slop identity is
visible by absence); the top line reading FREE WRITE first is the
thesis in typography.

## PASS TWO — opposition and trims

**Skeptic-writer:** *Hidden tools mid-flow?* — The grip is visible and
constant, olive when open, shortcut-bound; HB-arc's blur-reveal will
teach it at first run. *Two left pop-outs will confuse.* — Distinct
edges, distinct jobs: screen's edge = the desk (drawer: identity,
places), paper's edge = the hand (sliver: tools). Never both open by
default; both obey the one vanishing law. *The glow will nag the
goal-missers.* — No goal, no glow; the goal is private, default modest,
one click to clear; the glow only ever adds warmth — there is no
deficit state, no red, no dimming below baseline. *Wide-screen users
who want density, not calm?* — Calm is the position; density arrives
as the Wall (AB4), in its own track, when pinned.

**Growth-vs-brand tension:** Growth wanted the glow default-on and
vivid; Brand held the chroma cap. **Trimmed to:** default-on but
subtle, cap non-negotiable, vividness is a theme dialect later (Flux
already owns "earn the orange").

**Trims:** per-theme sliver dialects deferred to the theme arc; goal
management UI trimmed to one inline edit (no settings page, no
history); no per-page goals (one writer-level target); the top bar
gains nothing else (no breadcrumbs, no logo — the wordmark corner is
Volant's question).

**Canon-violation sweep:** clean, given the amendments below. The
sliver honors the ranges (it is *reach at the hand*); anti-solicitation
holds (the grip invites nothing, the goal never asks); olive/orange
lanes hold (grip olive-when-open; nothing orange at rest; the glow is
warmth, not brass).
