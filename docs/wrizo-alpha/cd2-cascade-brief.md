## S1 — the strip (glance; persistent)

The far-**left** vertical strip, beginning below the top bar (Done
keeps the corner), never dissolving, focusable. Layers cascade
rightward toward the paper: strip -> panel (layer 2) -> survey (layer
3) -> paper. Four sections, hairline separators, icons + engraved
labels, olive on the open category:

- **A** — Journal
- **B** — Page · Plan
- **C** — Drawers · Shelf
- **D** (foot of the strip) — Settings (site-wide) · Change Theme

Settings here is SITE-WIDE, distinct by law from the sliver-foot
gear (page/writing customization). Nothing else joins the strip
without ratification (A11).

## S2 — layer mechanics (reach and survey)

Layers 2–3 are overlays; the paper NEVER reflows. Both dissolve on
keystroke via the one vanishing engine; the strip persists. Escape
walks back one layer. Layer 3 opens automatically from a layer-2
choice — never something to discover. At laptop widths the layers
cap and may overlap each other, never the text measure. Every
geometry assertion runs at BOTH reference widths (standing law). Open
layers may transiently overlay the sliver's grip zone (both
dissolve-adjacent chrome), NEVER the text measure; the grip persists
after dissolve, as does the strip.

**The dock.** Layer 2 carries a quiet close affordance; closing it
animates shut like a drawer (fence: ~180ms slide, reduced-motion
honored) and layer 3 slides left into layer 2's slot. The strip's
category stays olive (the survey is still its open reach). Reopening
the category slides the panel back in and the survey back out one
slot. **Vanishing-law rider (record beside A8):** a DOCKED survey is
the writer's deliberate word to keep it — it survives keystrokes,
dismissed only by explicit close, category switch, or Escape.
Undocked layers dissolve on keystroke as before.

**Small-screen rule** (supersedes the never-the-measure clause above
for TRANSIENT layers only): where the frame lacks room for strip +
panel + survey beside the paper, layers open OVER the paper rather
than ever pushing it — the paper's rect never changes, any width. A
DOCKED survey must never permanently occlude the measure: it
compresses to the available margin (fence: 120px floor) or the dock
affordance is unavailable below the floor.

## S3 — category panels (layer 2)

- **Journal:** Open the Journal (travel) · New page · a short recent
  list · "All pages →" opens the survey.
- **Page:** the Page face carried forward whole — title, star, home
  line visible; tags and the sending verbs (Move/Copy, Port) as
  expanding rows; the saved-silently footer. The left drawer's
  estate lives here now.
- **Plan:** Create a Board · Plot a Story · Open… (→ survey: the
  board list). Per-board rows carry Move and Delete behind a quiet
  disclosure — never first position. **Delete: one plain confirm,
  then gone.** Destructive color only inside the confirm.
- **Drawers:** the drawer list; choosing one opens its survey (filed
  pages).
- **Shelf:** the shelved list; survey previews on selection.
- **Settings:** what exists today (account/session and a home for
  future site-wide options) — invent nothing.
- **Change Theme:** the writer's AVAILABLE themes only (Plateau and
  Flux today), current marked olive, one click switches and
  persists. Future themes are ABSENT, not grayed — the visible
  locked door was ratified for first-run only; M1 holds everywhere
  else.

## S4 — the survey (layer 3)

Large thumbnails: title + two-line excerpt, or the card's image
where a card IS an image; current item olive; click = travel
(mounts in the main area). Drag-to-reorder only where an order
surface lawfully exists (a board's own cards — one-order-surface
principle). No counts, no badges, nothing begs.

## S5 — the left drawer retires

The fixed left drawer retires on framed surfaces — the cascade
replaces it in place (grid columns adjust to the strip's own,
narrower width where the drawer's track was). The strip's smaller
footprint (versus the old drawer) still feeds FX3's scaled paper the
freed width. Legacy below the gate byte-identical, as always.

## S6 — harness (`cd2.mjs`) + the park sweep

Strip present and persistent through every state at both widths;
four sections with separators; each category opens its panel; Open…
auto-opens the survey; keystroke dissolves layers 2–3 and the strip
survives; Escape walks back; paper rect byte-identical through every
cascade state at both widths; the theme panel lists exactly the
available themes and no others; the Delete flow on a fixture board:
disclosure → confirm → gone from list and store; the Page panel
carries the face's contents. **Dock flow:** closing layer 2 slides it
shut and layer 3 occupies its slot (position, not remount); the
strip's category stays olive throughout; reopening the category
restores both panel and survey to their original slots. A **docked**
survey persists through a keystroke (typed text does not dissolve
it) while an **undocked** layer still dissolves on keystroke as
before — assert both in the same fixture so the contrast is real, not
assumed. At laptop width, layers overlay the paper (paper rect
byte-identical to closed, at every state) and a docked survey
compresses to the 120px floor rather than occluding the measure.
Reduced-motion path: the dock/undock transition honors
`prefers-reduced-motion` (no animated slide, instant state change).
**The park sweep:** the left drawer's retirement falsifies cd1.mjs's
drawer-track geometry and face checks and others besides — park every
falsified check per A4, quoted verbatim, successors asserting the
cascade equivalents; enumerate the sweep in the fold's commit
message. Full suite green, both HARNESS_PARKED settings.
