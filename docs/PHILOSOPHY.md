# Wrizo — Philosophical Foundations & Architectural Law

**Status:** Internal architecture. Not a public manifesto. Never shown to the writer.
**Meta-principle governing this entire document:** *Show, don't tell.* The philosophy guides the build and never appears on a wall, in a tooltip, or in onboarding copy. If a writer ever has to read any of this to use Wrizo, we failed.

**What we are building for vs. building with:**
We are building **for flow** (the psychological state the writer wants — absorption, quiet critic, momentum) **using flux-inspired mechanics** (Deleuze & Guattari's process ontology — how we get the writer there). Hold both. Don't confuse them. *Flux* is how reality moves; *flow* is the experience we're engineering. The writer only ever feels flow.

**Definition of success:** A writer with severe ADHD who cannot finish a short article finds himself producing significant writing on a regular basis *without noticing the app* — after a learning curve that is itself as free of blockages and punishments as we can make it.

---

## The Core Thesis

> **Divergence is free; convergence is the product.**

D&G (and the source document built on them) give the writer permission to diverge — start anywhere, follow every tangent, swap the machine — and offer **no mechanic to return or to finish.** That omission is not a flaw to forgive; it is the exact shape of the hole Wrizo fills. ADHD is rarely a *generation* deficit. It is a *convergence and completion* deficit. So the philosophy, read literally, solves the problem our user doesn't have and worsens the one he does.

Wrizo is therefore **the invisible convergence engine the philosophy omits.** Not an outline tool. A machine where structure *emerges* from arranging fragments the writer already wrote — experienced as gathering, never as outlining. Everything else in this document is in service of this one job.

---

## The Two Minds (Dual-Path)

Principle 1 generalizes. "Divergence is free; convergence is the product" is the emergence-first (ADHD-leaning) case. The general law:

> **The writer's natural move is free; the resisted move is the product.**

Wrizo serves a cognitive-style axis, not a diagnosis: structure-first (top-down/systematizing) vs. emergence-first (bottom-up/associative). These are tendencies on a continuum, and the overlap population (AuDHD) is large — the same person may switch modes mid-session. We build doors, not separate apps.

Both styles share one root problem — a completion deficit from opposite ends:
- Emergence-first generates endlessly, never converges (a pile of fragments).
- Structure-first structures endlessly, never produces (a perfect outline, no prose).

Wrizo's job for both is identical: get them to finished writing despite their style's trap, by supplying the move that style resists.
- Emergence-first resists convergence → the app converges (Gather, bridges).
- Structure-first resists production → the app lets them build the frame, then pushes gently into producing and protects that production from perfectionism.

One substrate (the fragment-graph), traversed in opposite directions. An outline is a spine built before the fragments are filled; a rhizome is fragments generated before the spine is found. Two doors:
- **The Middle Door** (emergence-first): drop into a fragment field, write from heat.
- **The Trellis** (structure-first): theme → principles → points → write into slots. The trellis is a support for growth, never a cage; tangents must always be able to escape the frame into a loose fragment (the line of flight survives the outline).

**Forward-only, both minds:** forward-only means no-ERASURE, not no-REORDERING. The skeleton is clay (rearrange/relabel freely during structuring); the prose poured into each slot is forward-only (protects against perfectionist edit-stall).

**No-failure-states, both minds:** never flag an empty slot as failure, but DO provide the order and coherence the structuring mind finds soothing. The structure-first reward is the structure cohering ("it resolved"), not the emergence-first surprise ("I wrote that?"). Same no-judgment law; different felt reward.

## Architectural Principles (Law)

1. **Divergence is free; convergence is the product.** The irreducible job. Every other principle serves it.
2. **Mechanics over mantras.** Never instruct the writer to do the thing. Build a machine where the thing is the path of least resistance. *Forward-only is the exemplar — extend the pattern everywhere.*
3. **The return path is sacred.** Every divergence must be costlessly returnable-to. Tangents are safe *because* return is free. The app holds the rope.
4. **The app is the stable assemblage.** Provide novelty *inside* the container so the writer never leaves to find it. Variation is a feature; tool-churn is the enemy. (Novelty-seeking — "I'm stuck, I need a new app/room/setup" — is the ADHD procrastination engine. We satisfy the itch in-house.)
5. **No failure states, ever.** Reframe every stall as *environmental*, never *characterological*. No broken streaks, no missed-goal red numbers, no "you've been idle." The instant the app becomes a judge it becomes visible — and for a rejection-sensitive writer, punishing.
6. **Scaffold, don't supplant.** Build the writer's capacity and fade as he grows. Aim is **graduation, not retention.** Be the physical therapist, not the crutch. Wrizo aims to be *needed less*. That is what "amplify human writers" actually means.
7. **Voice is amplified, never normalized.** Hard law for every AI surface. The instant the AI smooths toward a competent average, it *is* the slop we are built against.
8. **The interface recedes in the act of writing.** Invisibility = below the attention threshold. Every modal, notification, or mid-flow decision yanks the writer back to tool-awareness and breaks flow. **Decisions live at the edges, never the middle.** *A genuine pause is itself an edge:* a nudge may surface on its own after real idle (never mid-keystroke — the idle timer resets on every key), up to a small cap, then holds. That is why an idle nudge is permitted where a mid-flow one is forbidden — it meets the writer in the gap, not the current. (This refines the earlier "nudges are manual only / nothing auto-rotates" guardrail: surfacing-on-idle is allowed; an attention-seeking carousel is still not.)
9. **A pen never types; a key never inks.** The surface has two independent layers. The text layer is the keyboard's and obeys forward-only — striking a word is a keyboard act, and only the keyboard can do it. The ink layer is the stylus's and is pure overlay: strokes rest on top of the words the way pen rests on a printed page, never altering, striking, or moving them. Authorship and annotation don't share a channel.

## How AI Is Employed (Rules)

- **Tutor, never ghostwriter.** The words that stay are the writer's. Always.
- **AI demonstrates inside the writer's own text; it never instructs in the abstract.** Not "Tip: cover letters are formal." Instead: the writer's *own sentence*, mirrored back in a shifted register, offered, ignorable. He learns register by seeing his words in two registers — never by being lectured.
- **Prime directive: amplify *this* writer's idiosyncrasy.** Never regress toward a competent average. The AI's job is to make him *more himself*, not more correct.
- **Mirror, not muse.** The most powerful move is resurfacing the writer's *own* strongest phrasings and live tangents back to him — building voice-confidence from his material, never feeding him foreign material.
- **Silent by default; offers, never asserts; everything ignorable.** No suggestion ever requires a response.
- **Scaffolds quiet as competence shows.** Frequency dials down as the writer demonstrates he can converge / return / self-edit alone. (Principle 6, operationalized.)

## Principle #4, Rebuilt: Becoming-Writer (the source document's worst principle, inverted into one of its best)

The source said: *disappear, dissolve ego, stop worrying about your voice, be just the vessel.* Reject this literal reading on three independent grounds:

- **Least faithful.** "Becoming" in D&G is identity-as-verb, not loss-of-self. It is *active, productive, machinic* — entering relation, de-territorializing the fixed self into movement. "You are just the vessel" is the Romantic-mystic passive-channel cliché D&G wrote *against*. This is LinkedIn-Deleuze in its purest form.
- **Most risky.** Telling an anxious writer with RSD to "dissolve the boundary between you and the work" describes *dissociation as a goal*. Flow involves loss of self-*consciousness* (the critic quiets) — **not loss of self.** Conflating flow with depersonalization is destabilizing for our population.
- **Off-brand.** "Stop caring about your voice" runs directly against Wrizo's reason to exist. Our north star is the irreducible human voice against AI slop — the same reason orange can't be removed.

**Salvageable kernel:** quiet the inner critic and audience-anxiety *during generation.* That part is real and flow-aligned.

**How we achieve it — through mechanics, not instruction:** Forward-only, with a runway. Backspace doesn't erase — it strikes through, and the struck words stay on the page. The visible mess is the cure for the perfectionism that freezes the cursor. But it isn't a straitjacket: a short runway lets the word you're still typing drop a letter; press again and that whole word strikes; once more and the word before it strikes; past that the page holds and says keep writing — you can shape it later. Struck text stays visible but leaves the prose — counts for nothing, blocks nothing. You may erase the word not yet committed; never the words already set down. Shaping is a separate, later motion. The writing motion only goes forward. These *structurally* deny the critic anything to grab — the app embodies "quiet the critic" without ever uttering it.

**The faithful reframe (the load-bearing one):**
> **The identity is produced by the practice, not required before it. You don't need to *be* a writer to start; you become one in the forward motion.**

This dignifies the blocked beginner (no credential, no confidence, no settled voice required first) *while preserving voice* (the voice emerges through the doing, not despite it). Faithful to D&G, on-brand, therapeutic. This is why onboarding reframes from "Prove you're a human writer" (a worthiness test — off-principle) to **"you're already writing."**

## The Pharmakon (the answer to "isn't this just AI doing the work?")

Every writing technology is cure-and-poison. Wrizo's specific risk: a tool that removes revision-friction could *cure* revision-paralysis while *poisoning* the writer's developing capacity to self-edit. If the app does all convergence invisibly forever, the writer never builds the muscle and becomes dependent on the assemblage.

The answer is Principle 6: **scaffold, don't supplant.** Bridges, rope, and prompts get *quieter* as the writer demonstrates he can do it alone. This is also the complete answer to any critic who calls Wrizo "AI doing the writing": the AI builds capacity and recedes. *Amplify human writers* = make stronger, never make dependent.

## The Voice Wall (settles the paste-rail from first principles)

The paste rail was never about paste. It is about **whose voice.**
- External prose pasted *in* → imports a foreign voice → **forbidden** (same reason as AI-ghostwriting).
- The writer's own work flowing in, and finished work flowing out → *his* voice → **always permitted.**

Principle 7 ("voice is amplified, never normalized") tells you exactly where the wall goes and exactly where the door stays open. Implement the rail against *foreign-voice import*, not against the clipboard.

## Cross-Cutting Invariants (true in every phase, every screen)

- **No-failure-states invariant.** No streaks, scores, idle-callouts, or red numbers — anywhere, ever. Audited every phase. *Momentum-driven* means **intrinsic** momentum (the writing carries you), never **extrinsic** (a counter guilts you). No one on the build team adds a streak "to drive engagement."
- **Local-first durability.** Writing never blocks on the network. Café-wifi drops are silent. AI is additive and online-only; when it's gone, nothing breaks — because it was always quiet and ignorable anyway.
- **Latency tiers = the invisibility guarantee.** AI is tiered by latency budget, and nothing in the writing flow ever blocks on a network call. (Detailed in BUILD_PLAN.md.)
- **Fragments are invisible substrate during generation.** The writer experiences continuous text; the system experiences fragments. Fragment boundaries only become tangible during *convergence* (an edge-activity), never mid-flow.

## How To Use This Document

This is the source of truth for design calls and AI behavior. When a build decision is ambiguous, resolve it against these principles in order — Principle 1 wins ties. If a proposed feature requires telling the writer something, instructing in the abstract, normalizing voice, introducing a failure state, or interrupting mid-flow: it is wrong as specified. Rebuild it as a mechanic.
